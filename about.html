Study Projects

Engineering Classification of Soils at A Selected Location within The Chattogram Metropolitan Area (South Khulshi near Road No. 1)

As part of the Environmental Engineering (Sessional - I) coursework (Course Code: CE-352), I collaborated with my lab mates on a detailed study to assess the water quality of Chaktai Khal, an essential canal in Chittagong, Bangladesh.

Our study revealed significant insights into the water quality of Chaktai Khal:

  1. The pH of 6.98 indicates that the water is nearly neutral but slightly acidic.
  2. Turbidity was measured at 65 NTU, indicating a high presence of suspended particles. This was supported by the high levels of Total Solids (TS), Total Suspended Solids (TSS), and Total Dissolved Solids (TDS), with TSS accounting for 65% of TS, posing filtration challenges.
  3. The water contained 8.8 mg/L of free CO 2, aligning with typical surface water levels.
  4. Alkalinity (280 mg/L) exceeded hardness (28 mg/L), indicating temporary (carbonate) hardness that can be removed through heating.
  5. High concentrations of chloride and iron were detected, along with uncountable total coliform (TC) and fecal coliform (FC) levels, signaling severe sewage contamination.
  6. Elevated BOD and COD levels further indicate significant pollution from diverse waste sources.


Conclusion: The water quality of Chaktai Khal is poor, heavily impacted by sewage and other pollutants, requiring immediate interventions for rehabilitation.
